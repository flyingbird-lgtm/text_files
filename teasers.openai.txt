The Tricky Coin: Out of 40 coins, one is counterfeit and heavier. Using a balance scale, how do you find the counterfeit coin in just two weighings?

Answer: Divide the coins into groups of 13, 13, and 14. Weigh the two groups of 13 against each other. If they balance, the heavier coin is in the group of 14. Then, select any 12 coins from this group and weigh 6 against 6. If they balance, the unweighed coin is the counterfeit. If not, the heavier side contains the counterfeit.

Answer: Divide the balls into three groups of three. Weigh two groups. If they balance, the heavy ball is in the unweighed group. Weigh two balls from this group; if they balance, the remaining ball is the heavy one.

The Hourglass Puzzle: You have two hourglasses — one measures 4 minutes, the other 7 minutes. How can you measure exactly 9 minutes?

Answer: Start both hourglasses. When the 4-minute hourglass runs out, turn it over (4 minutes passed). When the 7-minute hourglass runs out, turn it over (7 minutes passed). When the 4-minute hourglass runs out again (total 8 minutes), turn over the 7-minute hourglass. When it runs out this time, 9 minutes have passed.
